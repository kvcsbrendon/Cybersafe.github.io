Assessment of the Risk Severity for Each Threat

Understanding Risk Severity and Assessment

  • Risk Severity Calculation
    • Definition: Risk severity is determined by the probability of a threat occurring multiplied by the expected impact level or value of the loss.
    • Severity Levels: Risk severity can be categorized as low, medium, high, or extreme.
    • Probability of Threat Occurring:
      • Unlikely: Approximately every year.
      • Likely: Approximately every week or month.
      • Very Likely: Approximately once or more a day.
    • Impact Level/Value of the Loss:
      • Minor: Minimal impact.
      • Moderate: Noticeable impact.
      • Major: Significant impact.
  • Risk Severity Matrix
    • Matrix Structure: A matrix to evaluate risk severity based on the probability of threat occurrence and impact level/value of the loss.
    • Matrix Example:
      Probability of Threat Occurring Minor Moderate Major
      Very Likely Medium High Extreme
      Likely Low Medium High
      Unlikely Low Low Medium
  • Risk Assessment Approach
    • System Design and Operation: Risk assessments are conducted during system design (review), at regular intervals during operation (audit), and after a security breach.
    • Constant Threats: Regular assessments are necessary due to the evolving nature of threats.
    • Assessment Method:
      • Identify Possible Threats: Assess the probability of different threats occurring.
      • Assess Vulnerabilities: Evaluate system vulnerabilities to specific threats.
      • Assess Impact Level/Value: Determine the potential impact level or value of the loss.
      • Determine Risk Severity: Classify the risk as low, medium, high, or extreme.
